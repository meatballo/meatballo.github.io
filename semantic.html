Semantics of Form: Clam


A concept object inspired by the elegant, fast form of clams. Created with a focus on craft, material, visual harmony, and human interaction.

A functional model paired with a form model, this project was an introductory exploration in affordance and form. Definitely a more experimental, but fun, project!

Time

 2 weeks

Role

 ID

Tools

 Foam, Gesso, Spackle, Balsa Wood, Rubber Bands, Clay

Project Outline


This project is very conceptual, we are to create a mechanical model, then a form/visual model based off of the mechanical one. It is an introduction to physical prototyping while also allowing us creative freedom.

Playing with Wood Interactions


With rubber bands and balsa wood, I explored some simple wood and rubber interactions, balancing between practical and play. Excuse the most playful one at the end :>

Prototyping


Going with the 1st wood one, the clapping mechanism, I developed both parts of the object separately to analyze hand-to-object interactions closely.

Final Form Model


I struggled with making the two parts harmonious, but I found that having two continuous curves in the overall form combined the two. This was inspired by the marine forms of the geoduck and clam. To add, the prior ergonomic tests make this form comfortable to hold. For example, the finger press area is slightly concave so that the flick is satisfying.


In all, a fun project in messing around with form.
