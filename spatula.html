Flowform Spatula

A beechwood spatula designed with fair curves and hand interation in mind.

As an avid egg enjoyer, this spatula is designed specifically for egg flipping. This design involves focus on ergonomics and craftsmanship.

Time

 3 weeks

Role

 ID, Ergonomics

Tools

 SolidWorks, bandsaw, machine sanders

Project + Interaction


A beechwood spatula only made of fair curves to develop a sensitivity to form. The design involves thrusting under the egg, lifting, then rotating the wrist and arm to flip.

Ideation


After observations of some current spatulas and a motion breakdown, I began ideating a spatula for fried eggs. I mainly focused on ergonomics and wrist positioning.

Prototyping


After finding a concept, I prototyped with foam to see how the handle and general silouette feels.

A flowform was first cut out of a block with the bandsaw, then secondary shaping was done to cue how the hand should hold the spatula.

Considerations


I focused on what is most natural to encourage the thrust & flip motion. The hand is tilted inwards to follow the angle of the wrist when thrusting & flipping.

Prototyping and testing with classmates helped me narrow down the form. The dramatic chamfer has the most visual noise, which suggests the thumb should be placed there. Additionally, the rate of curves fit to the curves found on the hand. The profile itself is curved to match the curve of a pan.
In short, the silouette and secondary shaping were driven by interaction.

Finished Spatula


After going through the whole round of machinery, I ended up running out of time and unable to get the handel exactly how I wanted it (it was a little big). The grey foam model is the best to hold, but the design’s affordances still show in the wooden spatula.

Wood grain added more movement to the form, which was nice :>

Overall, I'm happy with the result :>
