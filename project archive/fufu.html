FufuChef

Imagine a ricecooker, but for fufu.

This was a freelance project involving market research, UI, lots of sketching, and product visualization.

I worked in conjunction with another industrial design student, Elin, on this exciting project!

Time

 6 weeks

Collab

 Elin Zhou

Role

 ID, UI, CAD Modeling/Product Visualization

Tools

 SolidWorks, Keyshot, Figma

What is it?


Fufuchef essentially cooks fufu much like how a ricecooker cooks rice.

The fufu is slowly blended as it's cooked because it needs to be constantly stirred to achieve the right consistency.
Product Components
Lid (steam vent, water hole, locking mechanism, handle), UI, turning blade, inner cooking pot, heating plate, thermal sensor, power cord.

Some context: fufu!


Making fufu is a time-intensive, laborious process.

Fufu is a West African food that makes up the carbs of a meal. It's traditionally made of pounded, fermented cassava, which is then blended, cooked, and stirred until a desired silky consistency. Alternatives to cassava, such as oats and yams, have been growing in popularity.

A little market research...


There are no products that perform the same function as FufuChef, but we started by creating an image board of cooking-ware to analyze:

Recognizable Features
We wanted to maintain stylistic and mechanical conventions that customers would recognize + accept for a product they are unfamiliar with.
Cultural Considerations
Given that fufu is West African, we looked into cookware used in Ghana to find form inspiration.
Sizing Conventions
Similar to recognizable features, we wanted to maintain sizing conventions that customers would recognize and accept.

Very broad ideation


We started to explore general product profiles and forms. Our client had already conducted user research, so we took culture + habits into consideration: should the inner bowl be removeable? Should there be a "keep warm" function? Etc.

Defining our 'brand' to stay cohesive.


Our product sketches had a wide range of forms, so we decided on several branding adjectives that we wanted the product to emulate.

Primary Adjectives
Approachable, Static, Warm/Inviting, Honest, Timeless.

Brainstorming together :)


After establishing our brand, we continued to narrow down the form, making sure to check in with our client from time-to-time.

Looking into form cues, we made the form dough-like and soft to mimic fufu.

User Flow + UI Panel: what makes sense?


We opted for a digital interface as per client wishes, and focused on readability + understanding.

We looked into existing UI panels for similar products: ricecookers, air fryers, etc. to see how products balance the amount of control given to a user (set their own temperature + time or have auto buttons?).
Given this is a novel product, a more simple, automated UI would fit best to help garner user trust.

A little foam prototyping.


Prototyped the handle as it's in direct contact with the user. We found ideal dimensions and a shape that also matches the overall form of the product.

Ideally, we would prototype more for this project, but our client had understandable time constraints.

CAD Modeling


Through each iteration of CAD modeling, we found that playing with profiles/orthos aided in further refining the final form.

The final visuals + model were created for presenting the concept of this product, so specificities in the motor, heat plate, and electronics were not modeled, just considered.

Lid Features


FufuChef includes a lid with a locking mechanism and water hole.

The locking mechanism secures the lid on FufuChef, aligning with existing locking cues (righty tighty, lefty loosey). The lid also has a slight square shape to cue the user to lock the cap in place.
The vent hole allows for the user to pour more water in during cooking as per their fufu texture preference.

Final Thoughts


Color, material, and finish were mostly decided by our client as they had a clear idea on the look of the finished product.

I think this was a very lucky project to stumble upon - I love cooking (especially foods outside my culture), and I was able to collaborate with great people. This project was unfortunately rushed, given that our client had a competition deadline, so there was not much time given to physical prototyping.
If given more time, I would like to test sizing of the UI panel on the model as well as overall scale.
